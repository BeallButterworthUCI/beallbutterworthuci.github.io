A history in eras

2003 – 2026

The competition grew from a single-track software contest in the Donald Bren School of ICS into a campus-wide joint showcase that now graduates venture-backed companies, NIH-funded medical devices, and FDA-cleared products.

~2003 – 2013

The Butterworth Era

Single-track software contest hosted by the Donald Bren School of ICS, sponsored by alumni Paul ('74, '81) and Jo ('75) Butterworth. Prize tier: $5K / $3.5K / $1.5K.

2014

Inaugural Joint Edition

Donald R. Beall and the Beall Family Foundation fund the Beall Student Design Competition at the Henry Samueli School of Engineering. Software meets hardware in one combined ceremony.

2015 – 2017

IoT and Bluetooth

A wave of small device + smartphone + cloud entries. Tag Team's crowd-GPS Bluetooth tag (2016) shipped five years before Apple's AirTag.

2018 – 2019

Higher Stakes

Prize tier raised to $10K / $6.5K / $3.5K per placement. Eight-member teams. The first BBComp team to incorporate as a venture-backed company — CenSyn Inc., founded out of the 2019 Beall 2nd-place placement.

2020 – 2022

Pandemic and Recovery

2020 cycle cancelled due to COVID-19. 2021 ran virtually. By 2022 the ceremony was back in person with a $40K+ total purse.

2023 »

International Era

A Brazilian Collaboration Prize debuts, pairing UCI students with the Pontifical Catholic University of Rio Grande do Sul (PUCRS). Rebranded the International Collaboration Prize in 2025.

Why this archive exists

Institutional memory

Institutional memory for student competitions tends to scatter across press releases, the Wayback Machine, defunct project sites, and LinkedIn bios. The aim here is a durable, queryable record so that two decades from now a future student, founder, or historian can see, in one place, what was built, who built it, and what happened next.

Every team roster, every prize amount, every follow-on company outcome on this site is sourced to a primary citation in the open-source archive repository. Spot a missing team member or a bad link? Open an issue.
