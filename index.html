A history in eras

2003 - 2026

It started as a single-track software contest in the Donald Bren School of ICS. Over twenty years it grew into a campus-wide joint showcase that now graduates venture-backed companies, NIH-funded medical devices and FDA-cleared products.

~2003 - 2013

The Butterworth Era

Single-track software contest hosted by the Donald Bren School of ICS, sponsored by alumni Paul ('74, '81) and Jo ('75) Butterworth. Prize tier: $5K / $3.5K / $1.5K.

2014

Inaugural Joint Edition

Donald R. Beall and the Beall Family Foundation fund the Beall Student Design Competition at the Henry Samueli School of Engineering. Software meets hardware in one combined ceremony.

2015 - 2017

IoT and Bluetooth

A wave of small device + smartphone + cloud entries. Tag Team's crowd-GPS Bluetooth tag (2016) shipped five years before Apple's AirTag.

2018 - 2019

Higher Stakes

Prize tier raised to $10K / $6.5K / $3.5K per placement. Eight-member teams. The first BBComp team to incorporate as a venture-backed company - CenSyn Inc., founded out of the 2019 Beall 2nd-place placement.

2020 - 2022

Pandemic and Recovery

2020 cycle cancelled due to COVID-19. 2021 ran virtually. By 2022 the ceremony was back in person with a $40K+ total purse.

2023 »

International Era

A Brazilian Collaboration Prize debuts, pairing UCI students with the Pontifical Catholic University of Rio Grande do Sul (PUCRS). Rebranded the International Collaboration Prize in 2025.

Three threads worth following

From senior project to real outcome

Most BBComp teams are senior-design projects whose life ends at graduation. A smaller number turn into something durable. These three are the cleanest examples in the archive.

First M&A exit

CARGO Therapeutics → Concentra

The 2016 CeleriBio Beall 3rd-place team's lead, Louai Labanieh, went on to co-found CARGO Therapeutics. Concentra Biosciences acquired CARGO on August 19, 2025 at $4.379/share cash plus a CVR - around $170.7M in aggregate per analyst calculation. The first documented BBComp-traceable M&A exit.

FDA cleared

Makani Science (Khine Lab)

A bandage-sized respiratory monitor patch made from Shrinky Dinks, co-founded 2019 by recurring BBComp faculty mentor Prof. Michelle Khine with former doctoral student Michael Chu. FDA 510(k) clearance received April 2, 2025; $1.1M NIH NHLBI grant.

Commercial launch

Sentinel by Spotter AI

The 2024 Butterworth 1st-place driver-fatigue AI dashcam shipped commercially on February 17, 2026 as Spotter AI's flagship hiring, screening, and compliance platform for trucking fleets. The team also won the 2024 Stella Zhang NVC Grand Finale ($20K + $10K).

Why this archive exists

Institutional memory

Institutional memory for student competitions tends to scatter across press releases, the Wayback Machine, defunct project sites and LinkedIn bios. The aim here is a durable record so that two decades from now a future student or founder or historian can see in one place what was built, who built it and what happened next.

Every team roster, prize amount and follow-on company outcome on this site traces to a primary citation in the open-source archive repository. Spot a missing team member or a bad link? Open an issue.
