## Era 1 &mdash; The Butterworth Years (~2003 &mdash; 2013)

The competition's earliest cycles ran inside the Donald Bren School of Information and Computer Sciences as a single-track software event. Paul Butterworth (B.S. CS '74, M.S. CS '81), then well into a career that had taken him through Ingres, Forte Software, Sun Microsystems, Oracle, and AmberPoint, sponsored the contest as a way to pull a product mindset into a curriculum that mostly trained engineers, not founders.

The 2014 Samueli press release calls 2014 the "11th annual" Butterworth, and judge Kevin Kinsey's 2012 quote referenced "nine years" of competitors. Both anchor the inaugural cycle to roughly 2003-2004.

- **Format:** about 10 finalist teams per year, each with a faculty or industry mentor. Final round in Bren Hall. Single-track (software / ICS only).
- **Prize tier:** $5,000 / $3,500 / $1,500. Total purse $10,000 in 2012; likely similar across the era.
- **Themes:** vertical social networks (Portfelo, 2012), mobile-first utilities (M-Prescription, Approve), early IoT (SmartHome), photo/image understanding (Pictorria), indoor positioning (B.A.T.'s Bluetooth visitor tracking, 2013).
- **What's missing:** the 2010 and 2011 winners are not currently online. See the [2010](/years/2010/) and [2011](/years/2011/) entries.

## Era 2 &mdash; Inaugural Joint Edition (2014)

2014 changed the competition's structural identity. Donald R. Beall and the Beall Family Foundation funded the **Beall Student Design Competition** in the Henry Samueli School of Engineering, and the two competitions ran a shared awards ceremony for the first time. The combined prize pool jumped to $30,000.

This was the moment when "BBComp" started to mean what it means today: software meets hardware, ICS meets Engineering, code meets atoms. The inaugural Beall winner [Spero Diagnostics](/years/2014/) (sepsis diagnostic targeting <3 hours / <$100) and the inaugural joint Butterworth winner Sparky / Blueberry (Bluetooth-bubble passive networking app) set the tone.

**David Ochi joined as Competition Director that year** and has remained in the role through 2026.

## Era 3 &mdash; IoT and Bluetooth Years (2015 &mdash; 2017)

The mid-decade winners were all variations on the same architectural insight: a small device, a smartphone app, and a cloud service.

- **2015:** Roco's ML photo-sharing facial recognition (Butterworth 1st); MuTech's HIV diagnostic device (Beall 1st).
- **2016:** [Tag Team's crowd-GPS Bluetooth tag](/years/2016/) (Beall 1st) &mdash; a near-exact functional ancestor of Apple's AirTag, launched five years before Apple shipped AirTag in 2021. CeleriBio's bloodstream-infection diagnostic (Beall 3rd) seeds a thread that becomes the first BBComp-traceable M&amp;A exit.
- **2017:** Krobotech's affordable competitive prosthetic hand (Beall 1st); Memorlane's collaborative photo-sharing app (Butterworth 1st).

## Era 4 &mdash; Higher Stakes (2018 &mdash; 2019)

2018 raised the prize tier to **$10,000 / $6,500 / $3,500** per placement, the level it has held through 2026. The cycle began to attract larger, more interdisciplinary teams &mdash; CrewView's 2018 Butterworth 2nd-place AR/VR collaboration tool listed eight student members, the largest roster in the joint era to that point.

This era also produced the first BBComp team to incorporate as a venture-backed company: **CenSyn Inc.**, founded immediately after the [2019 Beall 2nd-place placement](/years/2019/) by Ayushi Patel, Andy Bhushan, and Trevor Silence. CenSyn went on to raise a $105K Berkeley SkyDeck Batch 12 cohort investment and pursue FDA 510(k) clearance for the CenSyn PenEEG.

The **AllerChain &rarr; forMED Technologies** trajectory begins here too &mdash; the 2019 combined-track finalist team returned in 2021 with a new product and won 1st place.

## Era 5 &mdash; Pandemic Disruption and Recovery (2020 &mdash; 2022)

The [2020 cycle](/years/2020/) was cancelled outright due to COVID-19. The competition pivoted to virtual planning and held a fully virtual 2021 cycle. By 2022 the ceremony was back in person ("back in full swing," per the ICS write-up) with a >$40,000 total purse.

The 2021 cycle is also when **Grasshopperfund** (Butterworth 1st) became one of the first BBComp winners to publicly enter an incubator (UCI Wayfinder) and a national accelerator (Blackstone LaunchPad / Techstars).

The [2022 Butterworth 1st-place winner Leprendo](/years/2022/) (online farmer's marketplace) became the second BBComp team to operate as an active commercial company. By December 2024 it had reported $400K first-year sales and a $250,000 USDA Farmers Market Promotion Program grant.

## Era 6 &mdash; Brazil and the International Era (2023+)

2023 added an entirely new track. The **Brazilian Collaboration Prize** paired UCI students with students from the Pontifical Catholic University of Rio Grande do Sul (PUCRS), facilitated by PUCRS associate professor and UCI visiting scholar Rafael Prikladnicki. The inaugural cohort: 33 PUCRS students, 40+ UCI students, 17 international teams. In 2025 the prize was rebranded to the **International Collaboration Prize**.

The [2023 Butterworth 1st-place team Eupnea](/years/2023/) (AI mobile / web app for tuberculosis diagnosis) followed up with a Microsoft Imagine Cup Epic Challenge American Region win and a Top 3 finish at the Imagine Cup World Championship &mdash; the first BBComp team to compete at a global stage.

## Era 7 &mdash; Deep Tech and Real Companies (2024 &mdash; 2025)

2024 was the year BBComp's commercial track record stopped being theoretical:

- [Sentinel](/years/2024/) (Butterworth 1st) is now the commercial product **Sentinel by Spotter AI** at sentinel.spotter.ai &mdash; public commercial launch February 17, 2026. The team also won the 20th Stella Zhang NVC Grand Finale ($20K + $10K) and placed 2nd at the C&amp;C Innovative Solutions SoCal Super Pitch.
- [NeoMold](/years/2024/) (Beall 1st) reached the Stella Zhang NVC top 10 and was a Life Sciences runner-up. Founder Christine Ly is now a PhD student at the USC BioMEMS lab under Prof. Ellis Meng.
- [Two Cube](/years/2024/) (Butterworth 2nd) won 1st in the Stella Zhang NVC Life Science track ($10K).

In a single year, all three top-tier joint-track winners placed in the Stella Zhang NVC.

[2025](/years/2025/) doubled down on AI-native medicine: Florence (Butterworth 1st - AI patient monitoring), AINGELX (Butterworth 2nd - SaaS-investor matchmaking, now NxRaise), NasoCoustics (Beall 1st - portable nasometry for cleft-palate speech therapy), VeinGuard (Beall 2nd - cervical collar that doesn't compress jugular veins), REACH (Beall 3rd - ML electrotherapy for stroke survivors). The International Collaboration Prize went to Oslo / Birdhouse by Oslo.

## Era 8 &mdash; 2026 (in progress)

The [2026 cycle](/years/2026/) is in its final week as this archive's most recent update. Demo Day &amp; Awards on **May 15, 2026, 10 AM &mdash; 3 PM at The Cove**.

Adjacent macro events shape the cycle's outcome environment:

- **[Vena Vitals](/companies/)** (2019 Beall 1st Qardiac follow-on) opened a new R&amp;D lab at Roseman University of Nevada Summerlin in March 2026.
- **[Sentinel](/companies/)** publicly launched as Spotter AI's flagship hiring/screening/compliance platform on February 17, 2026, with a March 31, 2026 expansion adding a digital driver application.
- **[CARGO Therapeutics](/companies/)** &mdash; whose co-founder Louai Labanieh was the [2016 CeleriBio Beall 3rd-place team lead](/years/2016/) &mdash; completed acquisition by Concentra Biosciences on August 19, 2025 at $4.379/share + CVR (around $170.7M aggregate). The first documented BBComp-traceable M&amp;A exit.
- Stacey Nicholas Dean **Magnus Egerstedt** departed UCI to become Provost / EVC at UNC Chapel Hill effective March 2, 2026; **Prof. Faryar Jabbari** (MAE) became interim Dean of the Henry Samueli School of Engineering effective March 1, 2026.

Twenty-two annual cycles after Paul Butterworth's first sponsorship and twelve after Donald Beall's, the competition has become what it was always meant to be &mdash; a place where engineers and computer scientists ship something, in front of judges who ask hard product questions, and occasionally start companies.
