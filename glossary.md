## The competition

**BBComp** is shorthand for the joint UCI Beall Butterworth Competition. People around campus also call it the Beall Butterworth or just "the competition."

**Butterworth Product Development Competition** is the software and ICS track. Hosted by the Donald Bren School of Information and Computer Sciences. It started around 2003-2004 as a standalone contest, before merging into the joint format in 2014.

**Beall Student Design Competition** is the hardware and engineering track. Hosted by the Henry Samueli School of Engineering. It launched in 2014 and immediately became one half of the joint BBComp.

**International Collaboration Prize** is the third BBComp track since 2025. It was called the Brazilian Collaboration Prize in 2023 and 2024. Awarded for joint UCI / PUCRS teams.

**PUCRS** is the Pontifical Catholic University of Rio Grande do Sul in Porto Alegre, Brazil. UCI's partner university for the International Collaboration Prize since 2023.

**Demo Day** is the final round of BBComp, held in May. Teams present their products to a panel of industry judges who select 1st, 2nd and 3rd in each track.

**Coaching session** is one of three or four small-group meetings between Demo Day applicants and the BBComp industry advisor pool, held in April and May.

**Workshop** is one of six early-cycle sessions (January through March) where teams learn product-development concepts like pretotyping, user-centered design, IP protection and customer interviewing.

## UCI schools and centers

**Donald Bren School of Information and Computer Sciences** (Bren School / ICS) is UCI's school for computer science, informatics and statistics. Hosts the Butterworth track. Named for real-estate developer Donald Bren after his $20M naming gift (announced anonymously in December 2003; formal school naming June 9, 2004).

**Henry Samueli School of Engineering** (Samueli) is UCI's engineering school. Hosts the Beall track. Named for Broadcom co-founder Henry Samueli.

**Paul Merage School of Business** (Merage) is UCI's business school. Home of the Beall Center for Innovation and Entrepreneurship and the Stella Zhang New Venture Competition.

**Claire Trevor School of the Arts** hosts the Beall Center for Art + Technology, which is a separate entity from the Beall Center for Innovation and Entrepreneurship.

**UCI Beall Applied Innovation** is UCI's central innovation hub at 5270 California Ave. Renamed from "UCI Applied Innovation" in 2019 after a multimillion-dollar Beall Family gift. Houses The Cove and Wayfinder.

**The Cove** is the co-working space inside UCI Beall Applied Innovation. Has hosted multiple BBComp Demo Day and Awards ceremonies.

**ANTrepreneur Center** is UCI's undergraduate-facing entrepreneurship program. Founded 2014. Funded in part by the Blackstone Charitable Foundation.

**Wayfinder** is a no-cost incubator for UC-affiliated startups, run by UCI Beall Applied Innovation. Several BBComp winners (Grasshopperfund, Nutripair) have entered Wayfinder after their cycle.

**Beall Center for Innovation and Entrepreneurship** is the Merage-school entrepreneurship center. Founded 2007 with a $6.6M gift from the Beall Family Foundation. Runs the Stella Zhang NVC.

**Stella Zhang New Venture Competition (NVC)** is UCI's flagship business-plan competition, sister to BBComp. Over $100K prize pool, five tracks (Business Products and Services, Consumer Products, Consumer Services, Life Sciences, Social Enterprise). The companion archive lives at <a href="https://github.com/StellaZhangUCI/History">StellaZhangUCI/History</a>.

**Blackstone LaunchPad at UCI** is a campus-wide startup mentorship program funded by the Blackstone Charitable Foundation. Several BBComp founders (Lily McGrale of forMED, Caitlyn Yang of Grasshopperfund) have been Blackstone fellows.

## People

**Paul Butterworth** is the UCI alumnus (BS CS '74, MS CS '81), enterprise-software executive and founding sponsor of the Butterworth Product Development Competition since approximately 2003-2004.

**Jo Butterworth** is the UCI alumna (BA Social Sciences '75) and Paul's wife. Co-leads Butterworth philanthropy at UCI.

**Donald R. Beall** is the retired Chairman and CEO of Rockwell International. Founding sponsor of the Beall Student Design Competition since 2014. Beall family philanthropy at UCI totals near $30M as of 2019.

**David Ochi** is the BBComp Director since 2014. Adjunct professor in both the Donald Bren School of ICS and the Paul Merage School of Business.

**Rafael Prikladnicki** is the PUCRS associate professor and UCI Visiting Scholar (August 2021 to February 2023) who set up the UCI / PUCRS partnership in 2023.

## Sentinel values used in the archive

When a piece of information cannot be sourced, the archive uses fixed sentinel strings rather than guesses:

- **Not Listed** means the source did not publish the value. This is common for prize amounts in per-year recap pages.
- **Undisclosed** means the source explicitly chose not to share a number.
- **No public record found** means we searched multiple sources and did not find the data.
- **UNVERIFIED** means the value comes from a single secondary source and has not been corroborated against a primary source.

## Acronyms

**BLE** stands for Bluetooth Low Energy. The wireless standard underlying both 2014's Sparky / Blueberry and 2016's Tag Team (a crowd-GPS Bluetooth tag five years before Apple's AirTag).

**BME** stands for Biomedical Engineering. A department within the Henry Samueli School. Many Beall winners are BME senior-design teams.

**EEG** stands for Electroencephalogram, or brain-wave monitoring. Featured in 2019's CenSyn (now CenSyn Inc., commercial) and 2023's Encephalopod.

**FDA 510(k)** is the US Food and Drug Administration's premarket-notification clearance pathway for medical devices. Makani Science (Khine-co-founded BBComp-adjacent spinoff) received 510(k) clearance for the Makani Respiratory Monitor on April 2, 2025. Syntr Health Technologies (2017 Beall 3rd founder Ahmed Zobi) is FDA-cleared per the company's site for the Syntrfuge automated microfat-processing system.

**ICS** stands for Information and Computer Sciences. The Donald Bren School. The abbreviation predates the 2003 Bren naming gift, when the school was simply the "School of Information and Computer Science."

**IoT** stands for Internet of Things. A recurring BBComp theme, especially in 2016 and 2022.

**MIE** stands for Master of Innovation and Entrepreneurship, a one-year graduate program at the Paul Merage School. Zarina Bahadur of 123 Baby Box was an MIE graduate (Class of 2020).

**MHCID** stands for Master of Human-Computer Interaction and Design, a one-year graduate program in UCI Informatics. The MHCID cohort regularly fields BBComp teams.

**NVC** stands for New Venture Competition, specifically the Stella Zhang NVC at Merage.

**SBIR** stands for Small Business Innovation Research, the federal grant program that has funded multiple BBComp follow-on companies including Syntr Health (NIH SBIR Phase I, 2017) and CenSyn (NSF SBIR Phase I, 2023).
