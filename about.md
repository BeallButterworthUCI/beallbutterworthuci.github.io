## What is the BBComp?

The **Beall Butterworth Competition** is an annual product-development showcase at the University of California, Irvine. It runs as two parallel competitions sharing one combined awards ceremony:

- the **Butterworth Product Development Competition**, hosted by the Donald Bren School of Information and Computer Sciences, running since approximately 2003-2004 with sponsorship from UCI alumni Paul ('74, '81) and Jo ('75) Butterworth;
- the **Beall Student Design Competition**, hosted by the Henry Samueli School of Engineering, launched in 2014 with funding from Donald R. Beall and the Beall Family Foundation.

Since 2023, an additional **International Collaboration Prize** has been awarded for joint UCI / PUCRS teams (renamed from the original *Brazilian Collaboration Prize* in 2025).

The competition is operated through **UCI Beall Applied Innovation** with day-to-day support from the ANTrepreneur Center and Blackstone LaunchPad at UCI, and has been directed by **David Ochi** since the inaugural joint edition in 2014.

## How the archive is sourced

Every fact on this site traces to a primary citation in the open-source [archive repository](https://github.com/BeallButterworthUCI/History). Sources include:

- the official BBComp past-winners pages at <a href="https://bbcomp.tech.uci.edu/" rel="noopener">bbcomp.tech.uci.edu</a>;
- UCI press releases (news.uci.edu, engineering.uci.edu, innovation.uci.edu, merage.uci.edu and ics.uci.edu);
- company filings, SEC documents and recognized journalism (TechCrunch, BusinessWire, GlobeNewswire, PR Newswire, Orange County Business Journal);
- peer-reviewed publications and conference papers, where relevant.

Where a claim comes from a single secondary source it is explicitly marked as such. Where we have *not* been able to find a primary source, the archive uses sentinel values like `*No public record found*` or `UNVERIFIED` rather than guessing.

## What is published here

This site is **public**, so we publish only public information:

- competition results: team names, project descriptions, prize amounts, faculty mentors;
- follow-on outcomes that are themselves public: incorporated company names, disclosed funding rounds, peer-reviewed publications, press coverage, university bios;
- public scholarly affiliations (lab pages, faculty profiles, etc.).

We deliberately do **not** publish: personal contact information, unconfirmed rumors and private business details. Anything not already in the public record we leave out.

## Contribute a correction

If you spot a missing team member, a bad source link, or a year we have not yet documented, please [open an issue](https://github.com/BeallButterworthUCI/History/issues) or a pull request on the [archive repository](https://github.com/BeallButterworthUCI/History). See [CONTRIBUTING.md](https://github.com/BeallButterworthUCI/History/blob/main/CONTRIBUTING.md) for the standard contribution flow.
