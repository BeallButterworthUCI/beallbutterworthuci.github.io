<h2 id="vena-vitals">Vena Vitals</h2>
<p class="lede" style="font-size: 1.1rem;">From a 2019 Beall 1st place senior project called Qardiac to a Y Combinator-backed wearable that gets continuous blood pressure off a skin sticker.</p>

<p>The 2019 Beall 1st place team was called Qardiac. Three UCI BME students plus their faculty advisor Prof. Michelle Khine pitched a Band-Aid-sized sensor that captures blood pressure continuously, beat by beat, without a cuff. The technology came out of years of Khine Lab work on Shrinky-Dink microfluidics.</p>

<p>Within months the project had a real company name (Vena Vitals), a CEO who had run product for two acquired digital-health startups (Ray Liu, who met Khine more than twenty years earlier as a UC Berkeley grad-school lab mate), and a Y Combinator S20 acceptance. Co-founder Joshua Kim, who built the underlying sensor as part of his UCI Materials Science PhD in Khine's lab, became CTO.</p>

<p>Five years later the company has tested the device on more than 600 surgical patients, anticipates FDA clearance within 2026, and opened a new R&amp;D laboratory at the Roseman University of Nevada Summerlin campus on March 25, 2026. The new lab sits inside Roseman Bioventures, a 120,000-square-foot life-science incubator that launched the day before. Vena Vitals expects to make commercial market entry in late 2027.</p>

<p>The roadmap beyond blood pressure: heart rate variability and oxygen saturation on the same sticker.</p>

<p><strong>Sources:</strong> <a href="https://innovation.uci.edu/news/vena-vitals/">innovation.uci.edu</a>, <a href="https://www.ycombinator.com/companies/vena-vitals">Y Combinator profile</a>. See the <a href="{{ '/years/2019/' | relative_url }}">2019 cycle page</a> for the Qardiac team roster.</p>

<h2 id="sentinel">Sentinel by Spotter AI</h2>
<p class="lede" style="font-size: 1.1rem;">A six-person UCI team built a working MVP for a driver-fatigue AI dashcam in a few weeks. Eighteen months later it became a commercial fleet-safety platform.</p>

<p>The 2024 Butterworth 1st place team Sentinel pitched a problem that has clean numbers attached: over half a million annual semi-truck accidents in the United States, the majority traceable to fatigue or distraction. The team built the MVP in weeks: a camera plus an inference stack that flags driver state for fleet managers.</p>

<p>Sentinel went on to win the 2024 UCI Stella Zhang New Venture Competition Grand Finale (a $20K Grand Prize plus the $10K Business Products and Services category), placed 2nd at the C&amp;C Innovative Solutions SoCal Super Pitch, and graduated from the NSF I-Corps Regional Program. One of the strongest cross-competition records in BBComp history.</p>

<p>The team's pitch eventually became the flagship platform of <strong>Spotter AI</strong>, the Wilmington-DE trucking-AI dispatch company founded in 2019. CEO and Co-Founder Peidi Wu (NYU Math and Econ; previously CTO at NaviData; co-founder of CabinFly) and Co-Founder, CTO and President Gabriel Stonys (University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign) launched Sentinel publicly on February 17, 2026. A March 31, 2026 platform update added a digital driver application that streamlines recruiting, compliance, and onboarding in one workflow. Spotter AI claims the new hiring and screening tools cut driver hiring and screening costs by up to 75%.</p>

<p>The original UCI team has gone in different directions since graduation. <strong>Spike O'Carroll</strong> joined SpaceX to build Starlink's payments infrastructure, also founded Dispatch AI (Berkeley SkyDeck Fund $25K grand prize at the 2024 UC Berkeley AI Hackathon), and built Libreddit / Redlib (his portfolio cites over 4 million monthly active users). <strong>Kevin Wu</strong> has done internships at Stripe (Link team), Mercury, Stoke Space, and Cloudflare (R2 object storage, January 2026). <strong>Kyle Deck</strong> is at Hybron after leading UCI Rocket Project (Liquids) for three years. <strong>Michael Ross</strong> previously competed on the 2022 Beall 3rd-place team Thrust Aeronautics, making him a two-time placement winner across both tracks.</p>

<h2 id="cargo">CARGO Therapeutics</h2>
<p class="lede" style="font-size: 1.1rem;">The first documented BBComp-traceable M&amp;A exit traces back to a 2016 Beall 3rd place team that most people forgot about.</p>

<p>In 2016 a UCI Beall team called CeleriBio placed 3rd with a bloodstream-infection diagnostic. The team's lead, <strong>Louai Labanieh</strong>, went on to become an Assistant Professor at Mount Sinai and to co-found <strong>CARGO Therapeutics</strong>, a clinical-stage cell-therapy company that filed for IPO in 2023.</p>

<p>CARGO did not become the next-decade success story its founders hoped for. The lead asset firi-cel ran into clinical setbacks and the company began winding down. On July 8, 2025, CARGO entered an agreement to be acquired by Concentra Biosciences at $4.379 per share in cash plus a contingent value right (CVR). The tender offer expired on August 18, 2025; the merger was consummated and CARGO delisted from Nasdaq the next morning, August 19, 2025. InsideArbitrage calculates the aggregate deal value at approximately $170.7 million.</p>

<p>It is not a unicorn outcome. But it is the first time a BBComp team has traced through to a publicly traded company that completed an M&amp;A exit, which makes it the cleanest data point we have for the actual ceiling of what these student projects can become.</p>

<h2 id="makani">Makani Science</h2>
<p class="lede" style="font-size: 1.1rem;">A Khine Lab spinoff that grew out of the same Shrinky-Dink microfluidics technique behind two BBComp winners. FDA-cleared in 2025.</p>

<p>Makani Science was co-founded in 2019 by Prof. Michelle Khine and her former doctoral student Michael Chu, who serves as CTO. The product is a bandage-sized respiratory monitor patch built using the Shrinky-Dink microfluidics technique that also underpins Vena Vitals. Khine reportedly conceived the wearable respiratory sensor after her newborn son had a collapsed lung that went unmonitored.</p>

<p>On <strong>April 2, 2025</strong> the FDA cleared the Makani Respiratory Monitor through the 510(k) pathway. Around the same time, Makani Science received a $1.1 million NIH grant under the National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute (NHLBI) program. The award is recorded in HHS TAGGS as R61HL177500, with $733,955 obligated to date across the R61 phase.</p>

<p>Makani Science is not technically a BBComp winner. It is a Khine Lab spinoff, and Khine is the recurring BBComp faculty mentor whose lab produced the 2014 Spero Diagnostics (Beall 1st), the 2014 A Hundred Tiny Hands (Beall 2nd), and the 2019 Qardiac team that became Vena Vitals. So the Khine pipeline is doing real work both through BBComp and around it.</p>

<p><strong>Sources:</strong> <a href="https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/makani-science-receives-fda-clearance-for-groundbreaking-respiratory-monitor-302417666.html">PR Newswire announcement</a>, <a href="https://taggs.hhs.gov/Detail/AwardDetail?arg_AwardNum=R61HL177500">HHS TAGGS award detail</a>. See the <a href="{{ '/faculty/' | relative_url }}">Khine faculty profile</a> and the <a href="{{ '/years/2019/' | relative_url }}">2019 cycle page</a>.</p>

<h2 id="nxraise">NxRaise (formerly AINGELX)</h2>
<p class="lede" style="font-size: 1.1rem;">The most recent BBComp team to pivot directly into a commercial product. Less than a year from competition to launched site.</p>

<p>AINGELX placed 2nd in the 2025 Butterworth track. Founder <strong>Kimia Yazdani</strong> is a UCI CS PhD candidate with a concurrent Merage MBA and graduate-research work in Dr. Wayne Hayes's lab on protein-protein interaction networks and multiple network alignment. The pitch: a personalized advisor that matches SaaS startups to investors based on due-diligence-level data.</p>

<p>By 2026 the project had been rebranded as <a href="https://nxraise.com/">NxRaise</a> at nxraise.com, with the tagline "the intelligence that powers your next raise." Yazdani is Founder and CEO. The site is in early access (v0.0.16 at the time of this writing) and positions itself as an efficient fundraising ecosystem for founders, investors, and advisors.</p>

<p>It is too early to call NxRaise a success or a failure. But the speed of the pivot is itself worth noting: a Butterworth 2nd-place team commercializing the same idea within the academic year is unusual in the BBComp record.</p>
